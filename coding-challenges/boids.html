Boids Algorithm

  Boids is an artificial life program, developed by Craig Reynolds in 1986, which simulates the flocking behaviour of birds. The name "boid" corresponds to a shortened version of "bird-oid object", which refers to a bird-like object (from Wikipedia).

  Instead of focusing on the entire flock, the Boids simulation specifies the behaviour of each individual boid. With only a few simple rules, the program's result is so complex and realistic that it is used in 3D animations in motion picture films.

The rules applied in the simplest Boids Algorithm are:

  • separation: steer to avoid crowding local flockmates
  • alignment: steer towards the average heading of local flockmates
  • cohesion: steer to move towards the center of mass of local flockmates

    • Some additional rules:
  • bounding box: prevent boids from leaving spawn area
  • multiple flocks with different behaviour
